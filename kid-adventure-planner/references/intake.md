# Intake and acceptance gates

## Interview sequence

Ask only one material question at a time. Skip facts already supplied.

1. Who is the planner for: nickname, age band, next grade, timezone?
2. What are the exact holiday start and end dates?
3. Which dates are travel, rest, or family-only days?
4. What repeated weekday rhythm should remain school-like?
5. What fixed classes exist, with exact dates, weekdays, start/end times, and course phases?
6. Which activities are flexible, and how long must each last to count?
7. Which entries earn rewards, and which are informational only?
8. Is makeup allowed? Does it require parent confirmation?
9. Is local-only use enough, or must phone and tablet synchronize?
10. What theme should the child choose? What colors, IP, or visual elements are prohibited?

When the source material contains screenshots or chat logs, first make a facts table with source, date, time, certainty, and conflict notes. Do not treat a promotional timetable as a confirmed booking.

## Written-plan acceptance gate

Present and obtain confirmation for:

- recurring rules;
- every fixed booking;
- date-by-date exceptions;
- task eligibility and minimum duration;
- travel/family/rest behavior;
- reward and makeup policy;
- local-only versus cloud-sync tier.

Do not write UI code before this gate unless the user explicitly requests a disposable prototype.

## Visual acceptance gate

Separate theme from function. Present a compact design brief containing:

- theme name and emotional tone;
- background and surface palette;
- typography and minimum readable sizes;
- card and button treatment;
- icon/illustration source policy;
- motion, sound, and reduced-motion behavior;
- phone and tablet behavior.

Do not sacrifice contrast, button clarity, or scrolling for decorative styling.
